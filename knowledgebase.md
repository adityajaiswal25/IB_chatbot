## PART 2: GRANDPARENT & PARENT STOCK
## ═══════════════════════════════════════════════

### What is Grandparent (GP) Stock in IB Group's poultry system?
- IB Group maintains GP flocks in collaboration with Aviagen to produce Parent Stock (PS).
- GP birds are the top of the breeding pyramid and produce hatching eggs that become Parent birds.
- IB Group ensures **consistent supply** of Parent Stock and ultimately commercial broiler birds.
- **Genetic superiority** from Aviagen's ROSS 308 AP genetics ensures better feed conversion, growth rate, and livability.

### What is Parent (PS) Stock?
- Parent birds receive GP hatching eggs and are raised to maturity.
- **Purpose:** Lay high-quality hatching eggs that produce strong, healthy commercial chicks.
- **Management principle:** Maximizing output through competent farm management practices (best farm practices).
- Parent flocks are managed under strict biosecurity and nutrition protocols.

### What is In-ovo Vaccination?
In-ovo vaccination is a method where vaccines are administered to embryos **inside the egg** before hatching (typically at Day 18 of incubation), rather than injecting chicks post-hatch. IB Group was the **first in India to introduce this technology**.

**Advantages of In-ovo vaccination over conventional vaccination:**
- More uniform immune response across the flock
- Eliminates post-hatch handling stress
- Reduces farm-level vaccination workload
- Provides earlier, more consistent immunity
- Reduces risk of handling-related injuries and contamination
- Saves time and labor at the farm stage

### What diseases are covered at the hatchery vaccination stage?
IB Group's hatchery vaccination (using Innovo® in-ovo technology) provides protection against key poultry diseases from Day 1 of placement, ensuring chicks arrive at the farm fully vaccinated. This includes protection against major early-onset diseases (Marek's Disease, Gumboro/IBD, Newcastle Disease — standard coverage targets for commercial broiler in-ovo programs).

### What happens at IB Group's Vaccine Research Lab (VRL) at APRDC?
The Vaccine Research Lab at APRDC (Aziz Poultry Research and Development Centre) focuses on:
- **Research and development** of inactivated poultry vaccines
- Targeting **emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases**
- Development pipeline: from **pivotal trials** → advanced research → next-generation (Gen-Nxt) solutions
- Goal: produce safest and highly potent vaccines for chicks and birds

## PART 4: DISEASE DIAGNOSIS & HEALTH MANAGEMENT (APRDC)
## ═══════════════════════════════════════════════

### What is APRDC?
**Aziz Poultry Research and Development Centre (APRDC)** is IB Group's dedicated research and diagnostic facility. Its vision is to be the driving force for transformative advancements in the Indian poultry industry. It is staffed by highly skilled scientists and researchers and operates at the forefront of poultry health, nutrition, and vaccine research.

### What disease diagnostic methods does APRDC use?

**Health & Diagnosis Research Lab (HDL) — Diagnostic Methods:**

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **HI (Hemagglutination Inhibition)** | Standard serological test to assess flock immune profile; detects antibody titres against ND, AI, IB etc. |
| **ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay)** | Advanced quantitative serology; measures specific antibody levels against IBD, ILT, MG, MS etc. |
| **Agglutination Test** | Rapid slide/plate test for Mycoplasma, Salmonella detection |
| **PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)** | Genomic detection of viral and bacterial DNA/RNA; rapid, highly specific pathogen identification |
| **Nucleic Acid Detection** | Advanced genomic test for precise confirmation of poultry pathogens |
| **Cell Culture & Egg Inoculation** | Isolation and identification of causative infectious viruses |
| **Bacterial Culture** | Advanced rapid methods to isolate and identify causative bacteria |
| **Histopathology** | Tissue-level examination for confirmatory pathological patterns of emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases |

### What pathogens / diseases does APRDC monitor?
APRDC monitors and diagnoses all major poultry pathogens including:
- **Viral:** Newcastle Disease (ND), Infectious Bronchitis (IB), Infectious Bursal Disease (IBD/Gumboro), Marek's Disease, Avian Influenza (AI), Infectious Laryngotracheitis (ILT)
- **Bacterial:** Salmonella, Clostridium, Mycoplasma, E. coli and other emerging pathogens
- **Emerging/Re-emerging diseases** tracked via histopathology and genomic testing

### What is the Environment Monitoring System (EMS) at APRDC?
APRDC's Environment Monitoring System (EMS) rigorously monitors all environmental matrices (air, water, litter/bedding) for microbial quality in:
- Egg storage and incubation areas
- Chick holding zones
- Grow-out farm environments

EMS continuously develops novel, data-driven systems to ensure the best hygiene and microbial safety for egg, chick, and bird production. All environmental matrices are precisely assessed for their impact on growth and production.

### What does the Feed, Food & Water Microbiology unit do at APRDC?
- Water is assessed against **stringent microbiological benchmarks** — healthy, enriched water prevents disease.
- Feed microbiology operates on the principle: **"Good microbes are permitted, Pathogens are prohibited."**
- The microbiome of feed and food is continuously assessed.
- Ensures pathogens like **Salmonella, Clostridium** and emerging fungi/bacteria are eliminated from feed.
- Prevents disease outbreaks and ensures good immunity in the flock.

### How does APRDC support the farmer's flock health?
IB Group's broiler integration farmers receive:
- Regular **immune profiling** of their flock (HI, ELISA monitoring)
- Access to state-of-the-art diagnostic lab facility through APRDC
- **World-class analytical support** for disease prevention and management
- Qualified veterinarians available **pan India** for best-in-class service under Parivartan
- **24/7 support** from a strong network of service technicians

### What is IB Group's Broiler Integration program?
Broiler Integration is IB Group's contract farming model where the company partners with poultry farmers, supplying them with chicks, feed, medicines, and technical support, and buying back the grown birds. IB Group aims to capture **50% of India's broiler integration market**.

### What does IB Group provide to Broiler Integration farmers?

| Input/Support | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| **Chicks** | Fully vaccinated chicks (in-ovo + hatchery vaccination done before placement) |
| **Feed** | Superior quality ABIS feed from IB Group's own plants |
| **Technical Support** | Pan-India qualified veterinarians + trained supervisors |
| **Training** | Latest farm management know-how at Centre of Excellence |
| **Disease Diagnostic Support** | State-of-the-art APRDC laboratory facility |
| **Financial Support** | Aid in upgrading to EC (Environmentally Controlled) houses |
| **Digital Tool** | Broiler Technology Platform (BTP) mobile app |
| **Integration Agreement** | 5-year integration agreement for stability (under Parivartan) |

### What are the Growing Charges (GC) for Broiler Integration farmers?
- GC ranges from **INR 8 to INR 13 per kg** depending on farm category and house type.
- EC House farmers earn higher GC (minimum INR 10–13/kg) vs Open House farmers (INR 8/kg).
- This is IB Group's **best GC policy** in the industry.

### What is the Broiler Technology Platform (BTP)?
BTP is IB Group's SAP-powered mobile application developed for Broiler Integration farmers. It is the **first mobile-based poultry management app in the Indian poultry segment**.

**5 key features of the BTP Digital Flock Book:**
1. **Upcoming Chicks, Feed & Medicine Dispatch Info** — Farmers track all material dispatches from company plant/stores to plan farm activities.
2. **Farmer Onboarding** — New farmer onboarding done digitally via e-agreements; instant onboarding within hours; no physical visits needed.
3. **Lot Liquidation Process** — Online lifting, farm clearance, feed transfers, and lot-closing activities; growing charges auto-calculated and visible in app.
4. **Lot Financial Status** — Cost sheet and payment notifications visible in real-time to farmers on their mobile.
5. **Analysis Reports** — Daily entries, previous lot operational and financial analysis, week-on-week farm management trends.

**BTP Impact:** 25,000+ poultry farmers across India actively use the app. It reduces bird mortality, enhances productivity, and improves profitability through end-to-end farm management.

### Why is EC House better for chicken production?

| Parameter | Open House | EC (Environmentally Controlled) House |
|-----------|-----------|--------------------------------------|

| Temperature control | Seasonal/natural | Automated, precise |
| Ventilation | Manual curtains | Automated fan + cooling pad |
| Feed access | Manual/trough | Sensor-based automatic feed supply |
| Water | Open drinkers | Nipple drinkers (hygienic) |
| Disease risk | Higher (open air) | Lower (controlled microenvironment) |

### What specific farm conditions does EC House control for chickens?
EC houses control the following critical parameters for optimal chicken welfare and productivity:

**Temperature:** Diesel/gas brooders + cooling pads maintain optimal brooding temperature (33–35°C for Day 1 chicks, reducing by ~0.5°C per day) and growing temperature.

**Ventilation:** Ventilation fans + circulation fans maintain air quality, CO2 levels, and ammonia — reducing respiratory disease risk.

**Humidity:** Cooling pad (evaporative cooling) maintains optimal humidity; prevents heat stress and dehydration.

**Feed supply:** Sensor-based automated feed lines ensure continuous, waste-free, hygienic access to feed.

**Water:** Nipple drinker systems deliver fresh, hygienic water; eliminates spillage and contamination risk.

**Lighting:** Panel-controlled automated lighting schedules for optimal growth stimulation.

**Q: Which feed should I use for Day 1 broiler chicks?**
A: Use ABIS Gold Pre-Starter or ABIS Broiler Pre-Starter for Days 0–14. Both contain 22–22.5% crude protein and are formulated with Hi-Pro Soya DOC for superior amino acid digestibility. Energy: 2,950 kcal/kg. Calcium: 1.00%.

**Q: What feed do I switch to after Day 14 for broilers?**
A: Switch to ABIS Gold Starter or ABIS Broiler Concentrate 10% Starter from Day 15–26. Protein drops to 20–20.5%. Energy increases to 3,025–3,050 kcal/kg.

**Q: When do broilers switch to finisher feed?**
A: From Day 27 onwards until lifting/market. Use ABIS Gold Finisher, ABIS Broiler Finisher, or ABIS Broiler Concentrate 10% Finisher. Protein: 18–19%. Energy: 3,100–3,150 kcal/kg. Calcium: 0.90%.

**Q: Why does energy increase but protein decrease across broiler phases?**
A: In early phases, high protein (22%+) is needed for rapid muscle, organ, and immune system development. As birds grow, energy becomes more important for efficient weight gain. Lower protein in finisher also reduces feed cost. This is standard industry-proven broiler feeding program design.

**Q: What is the calcium content in layer feed and why is it so high?**
A: Layer feeds (Laying Feed and Laying Finisher) contain 4.10% calcium — much higher than broiler feeds (0.90–1.00%). This is because egg-laying hens need large amounts of calcium to form eggshells (each egg uses approximately 2g of calcium). Without adequate calcium, eggshell quality deteriorates and the hen draws calcium from her own bones.

**Q: Are IB Group's chicks vaccinated before delivery to the farm?**
A: Yes. IB Group was India's first company to introduce **In-ovo vaccination** using **Innovo® technology** in the hatchery. All chicks supplied to integration farmers are **fully vaccinated at the hatchery stage**, eliminating the need for farm-level vaccination.

**Q: What diseases does APRDC's lab diagnose?**
A: APRDC's Health & Diagnosis Lab diagnoses all major poultry diseases using HI, ELISA, agglutination, PCR, and histopathology. Key pathogens covered include Newcastle Disease, Infectious Bronchitis, IBD/Gumboro, Marek's Disease, Avian Influenza, Salmonella, Clostridium, Mycoplasma, and emerging/re-emerging diseases.

**Q: What is the feed for Gavran (country) chicken?**
A: ABIS Gavran feeds (Pre-Starter, Starter, Finisher) are specially formulated for Desi/Country chicken breeds. They have lower energy (2,750–2,900 kcal/kg), higher fiber (up to 6% in finisher), and slightly lower protein compared to commercial broiler feeds — matching the slower growth rate and nutritional needs of Gavran birds.

**Q: What feed should chicken retailers use to keep live birds healthy at their shops?**
A: ABIS Chicken Counter Feed is specifically designed for this purpose. It provides 3,050 kcal/kg energy, 17% crude protein, and maintains bird health during the retail holding period, reducing weight loss and mortality at counter shops.

**Q: What is the difference between ABIS Broiler Concentrate 10% and 35%?**
A: Both are concentrate products meant to be mixed with grains at the farm:
- **10% Concentrate:** Mix 10 kg concentrate with 90 kg of grain ingredients (maize, soy etc.) to produce 100 kg complete feed. Processed with growth promoters, vitamins, enzymes.
- **35% Concentrate:** Mix 35 kg concentrate with 65 kg of grain ingredients. Contains 45% crude protein in the concentrate itself, providing a higher protein punch per kg of concentrate.

**Q: Does IB Group supply medicine and veterinary support along with feed and chicks?**
A: Yes. IB Group's Broiler Integration program includes pan-India qualified veterinarians, trained supervisors, access to APRDC's state-of-the-art disease diagnostic laboratory, and upcoming chick/feed/medicine dispatch tracking via the BTP mobile app.

**Q: How many poultry farmers is IB Group working with?**

## SECTION 4: R&D — AZIZ POULTRY RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT CENTRE (APRDC)

#### What is APRDC?
Aziz Poultry Research and Development Centre (APRDC) is IB Group's dedicated research facility committed to redefining the future of poultry research through state-of-the-art technologies and continuous innovation.

#### What are the key research areas of APRDC?

**1. Poultry Health (Health & Diagnosis Research Lab — HDL):**
- Environment Monitoring System (EMS): Monitors air, water, and environmental microbial quality for eggs, chicks, and birds.
- Feed, Food & Water Microbiology: Assesses the microbiome of feed and food; ensures pathogens like Salmonella and Clostridia are eliminated.
- Disease Diagnosis: Uses HI, ELISA, agglutination, PCR, and nucleic acid detection methods; cell culture and egg inoculation; histopathological studies for emerging diseases.
- Vaccine Research Lab (VRL): Research and development of inactivated poultry vaccines against emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases.

**2. Nutrition R&D Lab:**
- Lab Phase: Lab testing of feed ingredients, nutritional content assessment, quality control.
- Field Trial Phase: Real-world validation on designated farms; monitoring bird health, growth, and productivity.
- Commercial Launch: Making high-quality feed accessible to poultry and aqua farmers after successful trials.

#### What was IB Group's landmark hatchery innovation?
IB Group was the first company in India to introduce **In-ovo vaccination** and the **Innovo® vaccination technology**, eliminating the need for farm-level vaccination.
